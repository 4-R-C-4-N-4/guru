### 0.2 What was cut (and the editorial principle)

Two sources were ingested in `da3ca85a`, reviewed against the source content, and
then cut. **PD status was clean for both — the cuts are editorial, not legal.**

| Date | Source | Tradition | Why cut | Commit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-06-04 | Yoruba-Speaking Peoples (A.B. Ellis, 1894) | `yoruba` (slug removed) | Victorian British colonial officer writing about West African religion. Patronizing register ("primitive"/"fetish"). No insider centering. Imperial-colonial framing. | `de21074` |
| 2026-06-05 | Aradia, or the Gospel of the Witches (C.G. Leland, 1899) | `pagan_witchcraft` (slug removed) | American man of English descent paying an Italian peasant woman ("Maddalena") to "extract from those of her kind" the lore he then framed and published. Class-colonial asymmetry parallel to (if gentler than) Ellis. Modern scholarship (Hutton, Mathiesen) holds Leland likely substantially authored the material attributed to Maddalena, so even the would-be informant centering is disputed. | `395bcae` |

**Editorial principle (now established for the project):**

> Outsider-voice primary sources on subaltern traditions are not acceptable in
> this corpus even when they are the only PD option. Better unrepresented than
> misrepresented.

**Operational implications:**
- Insider-perspective texts for Yoruba/orisha religion (Bascom, Drewal, modern Lukumi writers) and for contemporary witchcraft (Valiente, Starhawk, Magliocco) are all under copyright. Until that changes, the `yoruba` and `pagan_witchcraft` slugs stay unrepresented.
- The principle does not apply to translators speaking within their own cultural canon — Jowett translating Plato, Müller editing Sanskrit/Pali texts as a 19th-c. Sanskritist, Bellows translating Norse Eddas, etc. Those remain in the corpus and are not flagged.
- For future PD-only ethnographies on traditions the author was not part of: weigh framing as heavily as legality. Ask: did the author identify *with* or speak *for/about* the practitioners?
- Recorded in memory at `~/.claude/projects/-home-ivy-Work-guru/memory/project_no_outsider_voice_sources.md` so future sessions don't reflexively re-add Ellis or Leland.

## 2. Cohesion gaps (concept-coverage view)

These taxonomy concepts are **defined but under-sourced** — the single biggest
lever on cross-tradition edge density:

- `demiurge`, `numerical_mysticism` — defined, but their *source* (Plato's **Timaeus**) isn't in the corpus.
- `anamnesis`, `divine_madness`, `ladder_of_love` — Platonism holds only the Symposium; **Phaedo / Phaedrus** are the missing primary sources.
- `apophatic_theology` — defined, but **Pseudo-Dionysius** (the urtext of negative theology, upstream of Eckhart who *is* in the corpus) is absent.
- `theurgy`, `cosmic_sympathy` — defined, but Neoplatonism is **Plotinus-only**, and Plotinus is famously *anti*-theurgy. The pro-theurgy source (**Iamblichus**) is missing, so the concept floats unsupported.
- `sunyata_emptiness` — defined, but Buddhism holds only the Diamond Sutra. The **Heart Sutra** is the single most concentrated source for it.
- `fana_annihilation`, `divine_intoxication`, `separation_from_source` — Sufi-anchored, but only Masnavi Book I is held.
- `dark_night` — a concept *named after* John of the Cross's text, which is **not in the corpus** (and has a host problem — see §6).
- `theosis_deification` — Eastern-Orthodox deification; no Orthodox/Dionysian source present.

Pagan cohesion is the other prize: a Norse layer wires `frashokereti` (Zoroastrian
renovation) ↔ `apocatastasis` (Stoic/Origenist recurrence) ↔ **Ragnarök** (Norse
world-death-and-rebirth) into a three-tradition eschatology cluster, and ties
**runes** to `sacred_names` / `letters_of_creation`.

### 3.1 Plato — *Timaeus* (platonism)
- **Source / license:** Benjamin Jowett, 1871. Clean US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** `https://www.sacred-texts.com/cla/plato/timaeus.htm` — single page (~188 KB), one `<PRE>` block of continuous speaker-tagged prose. **No in-line section markers.**
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`paragraph-group`**, `paragraphs_per_chunk ≈ 6–8` (~800-tok target). Pre-strip the trailing `[N]` footnote block. *(regex-section-split is NOT viable — there are no numbered markers in the `<PRE>` body.)*
- **Cohesion:** The prime non-Abrahamic creator-craftsman + world-soul source. Directly backs `demiurge` (upstream of the Gnostic demiurge already in corpus), `numerical_mysticism`, and Hermetic/Neoplatonic cosmogony. **Highest single-text ROI in this document.**

### 3.2 Plato — *Phaedo* (platonism)
- **Source / license:** Jowett, 1871. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** `https://www.sacred-texts.com/cla/plato/phaedo.htm` — single `<PRE>` page (~155 KB), continuous dialogue.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`paragraph-group`**, `paragraphs_per_chunk ≈ 6–8`.
- **Cohesion:** Backs `anamnesis` (recollection), `body_as_obstacle` (body-as-tomb), `soul_migration` — bridges Orphic/Pythagorean transmigration (already in `greek_mystery`) to Gnostic soul-imprisonment.

### 3.3 Plato — *Phaedrus* (platonism)
- **Source / license:** Jowett, 1871. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** `https://www.sacred-texts.com/cla/plato/phaedrus.htm` — single `<PRE>` page (~133 KB).
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`paragraph-group`**, `paragraphs_per_chunk ≈ 6–8`.

### 3.4 Plato — *Republic*, the Cave + the Good (platonism)
- **Source / license:** Jowett, 1871. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** index `https://www.sacred-texts.com/cla/plato/rep/index.htm`; paginated `repBBSS.htm` (BB=book, SS=section). Form of the Good = Book VI (`rep0601.htm` contains "idea of good"); Allegory of the Cave = Book VII (`rep0700.htm`, confirmed: den/shadows/prisoners/chained). These pages use real `<p>` tags.
- **Acquire / chunk:** Targeted — `format = "html"` on the ~4 Books VI–VII pages → **`paragraph-group`** (the pages are large). *(Whole-Republic alternative: `html_multi` over the index + `page-as-chunk`, ~38 pages — heavier than needed for the Cave/Good focus.)*
- **Cohesion:** The Cave is the canonical cross-tradition illusion/awakening node — wires to Gnostic "sleep," Buddhist ignorance, and Ouspensky's `mechanical_humanity` already in `western_esoteric`.

### 3.5 Pseudo-Dionysius — *Mystical Theology* + *Divine Names* (christian_mysticism)
- **Source / license:** C.E. Rolt, SPCK 1920 (translator d. 1917). Clean US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** index `https://www.ccel.org/ccel/rolt/dionysius.toc.html`. *Divine Names* = 13 chapters served as separate pages (`dionysius.iv.ii.html` … `dionysius.iv.xiv.html`); *Mystical Theology* = one page, 5 short internal chapters (the "Divine Gloom" / affirmative-vs-negative core).
- **Acquire / chunk:** Divine Names → `format = "html_multi"` + **`page-as-chunk`** (one chapter per page). Mystical Theology → `format = "html"` + **`regex-section-split`** on `CHAPTER I…V`.
- **Cohesion:** The **urtext of `apophatic_theology`** and a major `emanation_hierarchy` source — sits exactly between the Neoplatonism already held (Plotinus) and the Christian mystics already held (Eckhart, Boehme). *Note:* the **Celestial / Ecclesiastical Hierarchies** are not in Rolt; take those from the **John Parker 1897** CCEL collection (`https://ccel.org/ccel/dionysius/celestial/celestial.ix.html`, pattern `celestial.<roman>.html`, PD) if wanted.

### 3.6 Iamblichus — *On the Mysteries* (neoplatonism)
- **Source / license:** Thomas Taylor, Dobell ed. 1895. Project Gutenberg states "Public domain in the USA." Clean.
- **URL / structure:** Gutenberg #72815 — `https://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/72815/pg72815-images.html` (single HTML file). Native divisions: Porphyry's *Letter to Anebo* + Iamblichus's reply in 10 sections/"books," each with numbered chapters.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`regex-section-split`** on the section/chapter heads (`SECTION` / `CHAP.` / Roman-numeral book markers; tune to Gutenberg's markup).
- **Cohesion:** **THE primary source for `theurgy` and `cosmic_sympathy`** — the canonical pro-theurgy answer to anti-theurgy Plotinus. Resolves the unsupported-concept problem in the Neoplatonism holdings single-handedly. Pair with Porphyry #77014 (§4.9) to capture the actual debate.

### 3.7 Heart Sutra (buddhism)
- **Source / license:** Max Müller, in SBE49 (Cowell/Müller/Takakusu), 1894. US-PD. *(Conze/ATI versions are modern-copyright — this is the clean PD route.)*
- **URL / structure:** Smaller (standard) recension `https://www.sacred-texts.com/bud/sbe49/sbe4931.htm` (~10 KB); Larger recension `…/sbe4930.htm` (~12 KB). Both confirmed to contain "emptiness" + the *gate gate* mantra.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`page-as-chunk`** (1–2 chunks total; grab both recensions).
- **Cohesion:** The single most concentrated `sunyata_emptiness` / "form is emptiness" source — the exact gap in Buddhism, in two tiny pages.

### 3.8 Dhammapada (buddhism)
- **Source / license:** Max Müller, SBE10, 1881. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** index `https://www.sacred-texts.com/bud/sbe10/index.htm`; chapters `sbe1003.htm` (Ch I) … `sbe1028.htm` (Ch XXVI) = 26 sub-pages, each with in-line numbered verses ("1. All that we are…"). *(sbe1002 = intro; sbe1029+ = the separate Sutta-Nipata — exclude.)*
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html_multi"` → **`page-as-chunk`** (one chapter per chunk) is simplest. For finer grain, **`regex-section-split`** on `^\s*(\d+)\.\s` within each chapter.
- **Cohesion:** Broad ethical-psychological backbone — `meditation`, craving, impermanence, the path; aphoristic register pairs with the Tao Te Ching and Golden Verses already held.

Chapter numbering in filenames does not strictly track chapter sequence (front-matter at ps003 onward), so the chunking should `number_source = "filename"` and rely on per-page extraction for the real chapter label.
  - **Horner path:** `format = "html"` → **`regex-section-split`** on Book + chapter headers; pre-strip Gutenberg header/license/footer boilerplate.
- **Cohesion:** The single highest-value gnostic primary source not yet in corpus.
  - Fills the **Sophia-fall narrative** — direct edge to `separation_from_source` and the Mesopotamian descent material (Inanna/Ishtar) already held.
  - Sources the **24 Mysteries / 7 Mysteries cosmology** and the **Ialdabaōth / Authades** demiurge imagery — major gnostic primary content the Thomas+Philip pair doesn't cover.
  - **Divine feminine** edge to Hermetic Sophia material and (downstream) to the Mandaean Drasha d-Yahia (whose manifest entries exist but never ran — see §6).
- **Recommendation:** Mead via gnosis.org. Reasons: matches an existing host/downloader pair (zero pipeline change), native chapter-per-file structure means page-as-chunk is trivially clean, classic edition is the most widely cross-referenced in scholarly literature. Pick Horner only if Coptic-translation accuracy outweighs ease-of-ingest.

## 5. Tier 3 — pagan / new traditions (the out-of-the-box ask)

### 5.1 NORSE — new tradition `norse` *(headline pick)*

The strongest "something out of the box." Both Eddas are clean PD on sacred-texts,
and Norse cosmology/eschatology slots straight into existing cohesion clusters.

**Poetic Edda — Bellows**
- **Source / license:** Henry Adams Bellows, 1923 (American-Scandinavian Foundation). US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** index `https://sacred-texts.com/neu/poe/index.htm`; `poe03.htm`–`poe37.htm` = 34 poems, one per page. **Völuspá** (`poe03`, creation + Ragnarök) and **Hávamál** (`poe04`, Odin's wisdom + runes) both confirmed. Stanzas marked `<p>N.`.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html_multi"` → **`page-as-chunk`** (one poem per page — the clean unit). *Caveat:* Bellows interleaves footnotes also formatted `<p>N.`, so a stanza-level `regex-section-split` would need a footnote-stripping pre-pass; page-as-chunk sidesteps this.

**Prose Edda — Brodeur**
- **Source / license:** Arthur Gilchrist Brodeur, 1916 (American-Scandinavian Foundation). US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** index `https://sacred-texts.com/neu/pre/index.htm`; `pre00.htm`–`pre07.htm` (8 pages). **Gylfaginning** (`pre04`) is the main mythological narrative; Skáldskaparmál (`pre05`). Continuous prose, no reliable in-line numbering. Gylfaginning alone is ~130 KB.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html_multi"` → **`paragraph-group`** (~800-tok; page-as-chunk would be far too large).

**Norse cohesion:** **Ragnarök** (world-death + renewal) closes a three-tradition
eschatology cluster with `frashokereti` (Zoroastrian) and `apocatastasis`
(Stoic/Origenist). Odin's nine-night self-hanging on Yggdrasil to win the runes ↔
`forbidden_knowledge` / sacrifice-for-wisdom; **runes** ↔ `sacred_names` /
`letters_of_creation`. Hávamál's gnomic wisdom pairs with the Golden Verses and
(below) Hesiod's *Works and Days*.

### 5.2 GREEK RELIGION — new tradition `greek_religion`

Devotional/cosmogonic *cult* texts, distinct in register from the philosophical
`greek_mystery` / `neoplatonism` dirs but bridging straight to them.

**Hesiod — *Theogony* + *Works and Days*** — *cleanest ingest of the group*
- **Source / license:** Hugh G. Evelyn-White, Loeb 1914. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** `https://sacred-texts.com/cla/hesiod/theogony.htm` and `…/works.htm` — two single pages, each with reliable Loeb `(ll. N-M)` line-range markers (Theogony 88, Works and Days 80). *(Exclude `gtheo.htm` = Greek text.)*
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` (one file per work) → **`regex-section-split`** on `\(ll\. \d+-\d+\)` — dense, native, no footnote collision.
- **Cohesion:** Theogony = Greek cosmogony, direct parallel to Enuma Elish (`origin_events`, theomachy); Works and Days' Ages of Man = decline/recurrence parallel.

**Homeric Hymns — Evelyn-White**
- **Source / license:** Evelyn-White, 1914 (same Loeb volume). US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** `https://sacred-texts.com/cla/homer/hymns.htm` — single page (~148 KB), 33 hymns each headed `TO <DEITY>`.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`regex-section-split`** on the `TO <DEITY>` headers (one hymn per chunk; sub-split the long Demeter/Apollo/Hermes hymns on `(ll.)`).
- **Cohesion:** Direct devotional address to individual gods — fills the "hymn/prayer to a deity" slot the corpus lacks; pairs with Julian's hymns.

**Sallustius — *On the Gods and the World*** — *the bridge text*
- **Source / license:** Gilbert Murray, *Five Stages of Greek Religion*, 1925. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** `https://sacred-texts.com/cla/fsgr/fsgr10.htm` (single page; 21 short Roman-numeral chapters I–XXI + a `FOOTNOTES:` tail). *(Not `cla/eg/` — that 404s.)*
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` → **`regex-section-split`** on `^[IVX]+\.`; strip footnotes first.
- **Cohesion:** A concise 4th-c. Neoplatonic pagan catechism — the single strongest *bridge* item. **Edge case:** it's explicitly Neoplatonic doctrine, so it could equally **fold into `neoplatonism`** rather than `greek_religion`.

**Julian the Apostate — Hymns to King Helios + the Mother of the Gods** — *lower priority (extraction overhead)*
- **Source / license:** Wilmer Cave Wright, Loeb 1913. US-PD.
- **URL / structure:** **not on sacred-texts.** Gutenberg #48664 (*Works of Julian, Vol. 1*) — `https://www.gutenberg.org/files/48664/48664-h/48664-h.html`. Hymn to Helios = Oration IV, Hymn to the Mother of the Gods = Oration V.
- **Acquire / chunk:** `format = "html"` with **custom pre-processing**: the transcription interleaves Greek + English paragraphs, so a Greek-stripping pre-pass is required and the two orations must be sliced out of the full-volume file. Then `regex-section-split` on `[N]` section markers (or paragraph-group after Greek removal).
- **Cohesion:** A reigning emperor's solar-theology — the latest, most syncretic pagan voice; Helios theology ties Greek cult to Neoplatonic emanation. Julian was Sallustius's patron, so the two reinforce each other. Defer until the simpler Greek texts land, owing to the interleaved-Greek overhead.

### 5.3 Other out-of-the-box options (optional)

- **Bardo Thodol** (Tibetan Book of the Dead), Evans-Wentz 1927, `https://sacred-texts.com/tib/tbd/index.htm` — PD, `html_multi`. Already flagged in the manifest's deferred list. Would deepen **buddhism** *and* directly parallel the Egyptian Book of the Dead (`psychopomp_journey`, `funerary_navigation`) — a strong Egyptian↔Tibetan funerary-navigation edge. Caveat: Evans-Wentz's translation framing is contested, but it's the standard PD access point.
- **Jainism** (new tradition `jainism`) — Jacobi's *Jaina Sutras*, SBE22/45, `https://sacred-texts.com/jai/index.htm`, PD. A genuinely new epistemology (*anekantavada* — many-sidedness of truth) and rigorous `ascetic_discipline`. Manifest notes lower edge density; include only if you want a second new tradition beyond pagan.

## 6. Flagged / not recommended

- **John of the Cross — *Dark Night of the Soul* (host problem).** The PD David Lewis (1864/1908) translation is **only on archive.org** — not a supported site. The CCEL copy is the **E. Allison Peers (1934/53) translation, which is NOT public domain** (do not use). Gutenberg has no edition. This is a real gap (the corpus has a `dark_night` concept literally named for it); resolving it needs a deliberate decision to add archive.org as a pipeline source, or to wait for a Gutenberg edition. **Flag, don't acquire.**
- **accesstoinsight.org is a licensing trap.** Although a downloader exists (`scripts/downloaders/access_to_insight.py`), most ATI content is modern Thanissaro/Bodhi translations under **CC-BY-NC** — the non-commercial clause makes them unusable. Fill Buddhism from sacred-texts SBE volumes (§3.7, §3.8, §4.11) instead.
- **Sumerian *Descent of Inanna* — not cleanly PD.** sacred-texts' Sumerian set (`ane/sum/`) is Kramer 1944/61 (in copyright). The corpus already holds the Akkadian Descent of Ishtar; a PD Sumerian parallel isn't available on a supported site. Skip.
- **Babylonian penitential psalms / hymns — no clean unit.** They exist only embedded in mixed PD volumes (Records of the Past, Jastrow); no self-contained primary-text page to acquire cleanly. Defer; the one isolable item is the "Akkadian Hymn to the Setting Sun" in `ane/rp/rp202/`.
- **Hafiz *Divan* (Gertrude Bell, 1897)** — PD and clean (`isl/pdh/`, ~80 pages, `html_multi` + `page-as-chunk`), best `divine_intoxication` source, BUT the volume interleaves per-poem **Notes** apparatus pages; expect the tag-review step to reject those (consistent with the "reject tags on preface/apparatus chunks" rule). Includable, just noisy — Tier 2.5.
- **Ibn Arabi — *Tarjuman al-Ashwaq* (Nicholson, 1911)** — PD and on-site (`isl/taa/`), but each ode is **interleaved with heavy line-by-line commentary**; primary-source verse is a minority of each page. Lowest signal density of the Sufi options; most tag-review friction. Defer.
- **Pyramid Texts** — flagged in the manifest's deferred list, but the standard Mercer translation is **1952 (not PD)**, and Egyptian is already the corpus's second-largest tradition. Low priority.
- **Orphaned manifest entry: `mandaean`.** `sources/manifest.toml` defines two Mandaean John-Book sources (gnosis.org), but there is **no `corpus/mandaean/` directory** — it was planned and never ran through the pipeline. Worth resolving (acquire+chunk it, or remove the entries) since "the only living Gnostic religion" is a high-cohesion addition adjacent to the Gospel of Thomas/Philip already held.

## 7. Suggested sequencing

1. **Platonism backbone** (§3.1–3.4): Timaeus, Phaedo, Phaedrus, Republic-cave. Biggest cohesion lever; one tradition goes from 1 text to 5.
2. **Apophatic + theurgy bridge** (§3.5, §3.6, §4.7, §4.9): Pseudo-Dionysius, Iamblichus, Cloud of Unknowing, Porphyry. Wires Neoplatonism ↔ Christian mysticism and sources two floating concepts.
3. **Rescue Buddhism** (§3.7, §3.8, §4.6, §4.11): Heart Sutra, Dhammapada, Platform Sutra, SBE11 suttas. From 1 text to 5; sources `sunyata_emptiness`.
4. **Deepen Sufism + Mesopotamian** (§4.1–4.5): Masnavi II–VI, Attar, Shabistari, Gilgamesh, Adapa.
5. **Pagan layer** (§5): Norse Eddas (`norse`) first — clean and high-cohesion; then Hesiod + Homeric Hymns + Sallustius (`greek_religion`); Julian last.
6. **Loose threads:** resolve `mandaean`; decide on Dark Night's archive.org source-add; check the `sefer-yetirah` vs `sefer-yetzirah` dir pair in `corpus/jewish_mysticism/` (looks like a typo'd duplicate — verify before it skews counts).
